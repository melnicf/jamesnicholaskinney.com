## What This Project Is

JamesNicholasKinney.com is a modern, AI-assisted publishing and intelligence platform built around James' personal authority.

At its core it functions as:

- A **daily habit destination** for understanding what matters now
- A **curated intelligence layer**, not a raw news feed
- A **distribution engine**, where the website is the source of truth
- A **personal authority hub**, centered on framing and interpretation

The platform prioritizes:

- Aggregation before original creation
- Speed over perfection
- Framing over neutrality
- Distribution over ownership of audience data

AI is used to reduce operational friction, not to replace human judgment.

---

## What This Project Is NOT

Explicitly **not** part of the MVP:

- An email newsletter or email marketing platform
- A traditional newsroom or investigative journalism outlet
- A social network or community product
- A fully autonomous AI publishing system
- A long-form blog optimized for email capture or SEO farms

If a feature does not support **attention, habit, or authority**, it is deferred.

## Product Vision

JamesNicholasKinney.com exists to help people understand **what actually matters** in a world overwhelmed by information.

It does not attempt to be first, neutral, or exhaustive. It aims to be: **clear, opinionated, fast, and trustworthy.**

The long-term vision is for the site to be a **daily reference point** where people go to orient themselves before forming opinions or making decisions.

### The Core Problem

- Infinite content, limited insight
- Speed without context
- Noise disguised as news
- Algorithms optimized for outrage, not understanding

### The Product's Answer

- An **aggregation engine** that surfaces what matters
- A **human framing layer** that adds meaning
- A **distribution system** that meets people where they already are

### Target Audience

- Business leaders
- Technologists and operators
- Policy-aware professionals
- Creatives and cultural observers
- Curious generalists

These users value signal over noise, are time-constrained, already consume content on LinkedIn and YouTube, and want context — not just headlines.

### Core Product Pillars

1. **Aggregation Before Creation** — Traffic, habit, and relevance are built first through aggregation. Original content is selective and purposeful.
2. **Framing Is the Product** — The value is not in what is linked, but in how it is contextualized, why it matters, and how it connects to broader trends.
3. **Distribution Is Not Optional** — Content is designed with distribution in mind from the start. The website is the source of truth; LinkedIn and YouTube are amplification layers.
4. **Speed Over Polish** — Timeliness and recency over perfect presentation.
5. **AI as Infrastructure, Not Identity** — AI reduces manual work and increases consistency. It is not the voice or personality of the platform.

### Strategic Constraints (Non-Negotiables)

Locked for MVP:

- No email newsletters
- No email capture funnels
- No membership or login system
- No community features
- No autonomous AI publishing
- No attempt to "out-report" legacy media

## High-Level Platform Flow

1. Content is aggregated from trusted sources (RSS)
2. Items are categorized and queued for review
3. James (or editors) add framing and context
4. Content is published on the site
5. Selected items are distributed via LinkedIn and YouTube

AI assists with categorization, summarization, and draft copy for distribution. AI does **not** publish autonomously.

## Content Strategy & Models

### Hybrid Content Model

1. **Aggregated Content (Core)** — Sourced from trusted external publishers with added context/framing. Primary driver of daily freshness, traffic, and habit.
2. **Original Content (Selective)** — Analysis pieces, context explainers, trend breakdowns, opinion essays, evergreen articles. One paid writer per category as needed.
3. **Video Content (YouTube)** — Weekly synthesis recaps, not daily updates.

### Content Requirements

Each aggregated item includes:
- Headline (may be edited for clarity)
- Source name
- Original publication timestamp
- Short summary or excerpt
- James' framing or commentary ("Why this matters")

### Tone & Framing

- Opinionated but grounded
- Clear, not sensational
- Confident without being absolute
- Contextual rather than reactive

### Attribution & Ethics

- Always credit original sources
- Never misrepresent original reporting
- Distinguish between source facts, editorial framing, and opinion

## Editorial Workflow

### Roles

| Role          | Permissions                                                          |
| ------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Admin**     | Full system access, manages sources/categories/permissions, can publish without review |
| **Editor**    | Reviews aggregated content, edits headlines/summaries, adds framing, approves for publishing |
| **Contributor** *(future)* | Submits original content, cannot publish without approval |

### Content States

1. **Ingested** — Pulled from external sources, not visible to users
2. **Needs Review** — Awaiting editorial review, visible only in admin
3. **Approved** — Edited and framed, ready for publication
4. **Published** — Live on the site, eligible for distribution

### Aggregated Content Flow

1. Content ingested automatically
2. System de-duplicates and categorizes
3. Item appears in "Needs Review" queue
4. Editor reviews, adjusts headline, adds framing
5. Editor publishes

Target: ingestion to publish in **minutes, not hours**.

## AI & Automation Strategy

### Approved Use Cases

1. **Content Ingestion** — Normalize content, assist categorization, flag duplicates
2. **Summarization** — Generate short summaries/excerpts, reduce editor reading time
3. **Editorial Framing Drafts** — Suggest "Why this matters" drafts, highlight connections
4. **Distribution Drafting** — Draft LinkedIn posts, suggest headlines, generate YouTube titles/descriptions

### Hard Rules

- AI output is always a draft
- Publishing always requires human approval
- AI never speaks *as* James without review
- AI never fabricates facts or sources
- If trust is at risk, automation stops

### Deferred (Post-MVP)

- Autonomous publishing
- Personalized AI feeds
- "Think like James" AI agents
- AI chat in James' voice

## Definition of MVP Success

The MVP is successful when:

- New content is published daily
- Aggregation → review → publish flow is smooth
- LinkedIn posts are consistently generated from site content
- Weekly YouTube recaps are live and embedded
- The site feels current, alive, and opinionated
- Ongoing operation requires low manual effort

## Guiding Principles

> **If the platform disappeared tomorrow, users should feel they lost a trusted filter, not just a website.**

> **Does this piece help someone understand the world better in less time?** If not, it does not belong.

> **Can a first-time visitor understand what this site does in under 10 seconds?** If not, the architecture is too complex.
